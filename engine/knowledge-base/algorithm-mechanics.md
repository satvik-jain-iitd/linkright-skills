# LinkedIn Algorithm Mechanics

Engine knowledge. Generic to any user. The Distribution Strategist loads this. Time sensitive, the System Optimizer keeps it current, last refreshed 2026-06.

The feed runs on an interest graph now, not just a social graph. Relevance and demonstrated expertise earn reach, not just who you know.

## The signals that decide reach

Dwell time is the primary signal. The platform measures how long a reader actually stays on a post. A post someone reads for thirty seconds beats one with fifty quick likes. A fast scroll past tells the feed the post was forgettable.

The first sixty minutes set the trajectory. The post is shown to a small slice of the network, roughly two to five percent, in the first hour. Strong engagement in that window triggers wider distribution. Weak engagement caps it.

Comments weigh far more than likes, on the order of fifteen times. A comment is a strong resonance signal. Reply bait and a seeded first comment matter, this is why Publish and Engage is core, not a bonus.

Saves signal lasting value, design save worthy content for utility posts.

## Format and reach

Document posts, the PDF carousel, lead engagement, around six and a half percent on average, the highest of any format. Native video follows, around five and a half percent. Text with a single strong image still works for story and opinion.

External links cut reach hard, roughly sixty percent less than the same post without a link. As of early 2026, the old workaround of putting the link in the first comment is also penalized. Keep links out of the post, point people to a profile or a follow instead.

## How to use this

Pick the format that earns dwell and early comments for the topic. Favour document carousels and native video for reach. Open with a hook that stops the scroll inside the mobile fold. Design for a comment, not just a like. Avoid outbound links in the post body. Always check the owner's own performance data, their real results override the general rule when they disagree.
