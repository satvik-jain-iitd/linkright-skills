# Design Principles

Engine knowledge. Generic to any user. The Art Director and the Asset Builder load this. The owner's actual colors and fonts live in the brand identity file, this is the method.

## Hierarchy

The most important element is the largest and brightest. On a carousel cover, the hook reads first, everything else supports it. One focal point per slide or image, not three competing ones.

## Color

Use color with intent. A dark background with one bright accent reads as premium and stops the scroll. Reserve a second highlight color for the single thing that matters most on a slide. Read the actual palette from the brand identity file.

## Type

Two fonts at most, often one. Size contrast carries the hierarchy, a big headline over small support text. Keep type left aligned and generous with space, walls of centered text are hard to scan.

## Consistency

One palette, one type set, one grid across every slide of a carousel and every scene of a video. If a character or avatar appears, keep it the same across slides. This sameness is what makes a feed recognisable.

## Branding

A small watermark or handle on every visual, low key, in a corner. The brand should be felt, not shouted.

## Carousel specifics

Square or four by five for the feed. Slide one is the hook. Middle slides carry one idea each with short text. The last slide is a soft follow prompt, never an outbound link. Keep the layout grid identical slide to slide so only the content changes.

## The filter

Design serves the message and the brand system. A pretty slide that buries the hook or breaks the palette is a miss.
