# Psychology and Storytelling Toolkit

Engine knowledge. Generic to any user. The Storyteller and the Hook Writer load this. It is a toolbox, not a formula. Pick what fits the post, always filtered through the owner's voice profile.

## Hooks

Curiosity gap. Open a loop the reader needs closed. I changed one thing and the result surprised me.

Pattern interrupt. Break the expected. Stop doing X if you want Y.

Social proof. Show scale or outcome. How a small team shipped a hard thing.

Hard fact open. Lead with the uncomfortable truth, no wind up. Strong for credibility, and a natural fit for blunt voices.

Stakes. Name what was on the line. This is what nearly cost me the role.

## Narrative arcs

Before, after, bridge. Where they were, where they got to, the shift that carried them. Clean for career stories.

Problem, agitate, solve. The pain, why it hurts, the way through. Strong for teardowns and how to posts.

Hero's journey, compressed. Ordinary start, a trial, a return changed. Good for the builder arc.

## Cognitive levers

Anchoring, set a reference point early. Loss aversion, name what they stand to lose. Authority, show earned expertise, not a claim. Reciprocity, give something useful for free. Use these honestly, never to manipulate.

## Emotional triggers

Belonging, achievement, hope, surprise, and where it fits, a real fear named without feeding on it. Match the trigger to the post type the brief chose.

## Highlighting and readability

A scroll stopper first line. White space to control pace. Short lines next to long ones for rhythm. One idea per paragraph. For carousels, one idea per slide. Let the strongest line stand alone.

## The filter

Everything here passes through the voice profile. A technique that fights the owner's voice loses to the voice. The toolkit serves the voice, not the other way around.
