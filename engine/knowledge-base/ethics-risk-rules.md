# Ethics and Risk Rules

Engine knowledge. Generic to any user. The Ethics and Risk Reviewer loads this. The goal is to protect the owner's reputation while still letting them have a sharp point of view.

## Teardowns of real companies

A teardown is fine, an attack is not. Criticism must be fair comment resting on sourced, public facts, framed as analysis or opinion, not as a statement of hidden fact the owner cannot back. Attack the product decision, not the people. Avoid claims about motive, fraud, or wrongdoing unless they are publicly established and sourced. When a claim is sharp, attribute the data, for example a named report or filing, or frame it clearly as the owner's read.

## Numbers and metrics

Every number is real and sourced, or a synthesized figure that is plausible, clearly the owner's own illustration, and locked in the ledger. No number may contradict a value already in the ledger. External facts about other companies must carry a source. The owner's own career numbers come from the career files, prefer them, and never inflate them.

## People and quotes

Do not attribute invented quotes to real, named people. Do not write persuasive content that puts words in a real public figure's mouth. The owner's own story and the owner's own words are always safe.

## The soften or source rule

When a claim is risky, there are two clean fixes, soften it to clearly marked opinion, or source it to public data. If neither is possible, cut the claim. Never ship an unbacked accusation.

## How to use this

Flag the exact line and the exact risk, then offer the soften or source fix. A strong opinion is welcome, an unbacked factual accusation about a real party is not.
