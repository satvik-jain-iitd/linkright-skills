# Personality Primer

Engine knowledge. Generic to any user. The Interviewer loads this to read cognition from answers. It is a reading aid, not a test to administer.

## Why Big Five, not MBTI

The owner may think in MBTI terms, ENTJ, ENTP, and that is fine as a shared language. But for actually reading a person, Big Five is the stronger backbone. It is measured as continuous traits, not boxes, and it holds up far better on re test. Use Big Five to read, translate to plain words when talking to the owner.

## The five traits, and the tells

Openness. Curiosity, range, abstraction. High openness shows as metaphor, tangents, idea play. Low shows as concrete, practical, here is what works.

Conscientiousness. Order, follow through, rigor. High shows as structured answers, plans, numbers, caveats. Low shows as loose, improvised, big picture over detail.

Extraversion. Energy direction, assertiveness. High shows as fast, expansive, opinion forward. Low shows as measured, reflective, fewer but heavier statements.

Agreeableness. Warmth versus challenge. High shows as we, hedging, smoothing. Low shows as direct, blunt, willing to say the unpopular thing.

Neuroticism, or its flip, stability. Emotional reactivity. High shows as stakes, worry, strong affect words. Low shows as flat, calm, matter of fact.

## From trait to voice

The point is not a label. The point is how the trait shows up in writing. A blunt, low agreeableness, low neuroticism person writes short, confident, no hedging, owns the call. A high openness person reaches for analogy. A high conscientiousness person leans on data and structure. Note which traits are loud, because those are the ones the voice profile has to carry.

## Cognition beyond traits

Watch how they decide. Do they reason from data and friction, or from principle, or from story. Watch what they reject and why, that boundary is identity. Watch what they get warm on, that is where authentic energy lives. These are not Big Five, but they shape voice as much as any trait.

## Caution

Read traits from patterns across the whole interview, not one line. Do not over diagnose. The output is a short, evidence backed read of how this person thinks, handed to the Voice Synthesizer, not a clinical profile.
