# Post Templates — LinkRight Network

## Post Types

| Type | Signal purpose | Freq | Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| POT (Proof-of-Thinking) | Demonstrate product thinking on real companies | 2×/month | 200-300 words |
| PM Insight | Share specific framework/pattern/lesson | 2×/month | 150-250 words |
| Process Transparency | Show how you approach PM problems | 1×/month | 150-200 words |
| Short Take | Direct 1-3 sentence opinion on real event | As relevant | 50-100 words |
| Career Milestone | Genuine achievement (sparingly) | Max 1×/quarter | 100-150 words |

---

## Template 1: Proof-of-Thinking (POT)

**Research requirement:** Use the actual product for ≥15 minutes. Never write from hearsay.

```
HOOK (1 sentence):
  Specific non-obvious observation about this company's product.
  "[Company] made an unusual choice with [specific feature/decision]."
  NOT: "[Company] has interesting UX." (too vague)

OBSERVATION (2-3 sentences):
  What exactly you noticed — specific, verifiable.
  "Their [X] does [Y]. Most products in this space do [Z]."

TRADE-OFF (2-3 sentences):
  What they optimized for. What they gave up.
  "The bet is [rationale for their choice]. The cost is [what they sacrificed]."
  Show you understand WHY, not just what.

YOUR TAKE (2-3 sentences):
  What you'd explore differently and specifically why.
  NOT: "I think they could improve X." (vague)
  YES: "I'd test [specific alternative] because [specific hypothesis about user behavior]."

(Optional) SIGNAL (1 sentence):
  What this reveals about the PM problems you think about — if it flows naturally.
```

**Annotated example:**

> Linear's issue picker does something counterintuitive — it defaults to showing ALL issues, not "my issues."

> Most project tools default to personal views. Linear defaults to full team context. Every time you assign something, you see the full workload before you do.

> The bet: context reduces bad assignments. If you can see that backend is overwhelmed, you won't drop another P1 on them without thinking. The cost: it's noisier for teams with 50+ open issues.

> I'd test a middle state — default to team context but filter by current sprint. Same awareness signal, less noise. The constraint violation I want to surface is scope, not every open ticket.

## Template 2: PM Insight

```
OPENER (1 sentence — must be an opinion or observation, never a question):
  "Most [specific failure pattern] isn't about [common explanation]. It's about [real cause]."

EXPAND (2-3 sentences):
  Specific evidence or example — either from your own work or a verifiable public case.
  Never generic "I've seen this many times."

MECHANISM (2-3 sentences):
  Why this happens. The underlying force.

SPECIFIC FIX / TAKEAWAY (1-2 sentences):
  What you actually do. Concrete, adoptable. Not "communicate better."
```

**Example:**

> Most roadmap arguments aren't about priorities. They're about whose problem gets solved this quarter.
>
> Engineering wants predictability — fewer context switches, more depth. Sales wants responsiveness — the deal they just lost to a missing feature. Both are right, and they're incompatible in the short term.
>
> I've found the argument stops when you make the tradeoff explicit: "If we build X, we can't also build Y this quarter. Which customer problem do we deprioritize?" That shifts it from political to factual.

## Template 3: Process Transparency

```
WHAT YOU DO AND WHEN (1-2 sentences):
  Specific process, tool, or habit — and the condition that triggers it.

WHY (2-3 sentences):
  The problem this solves. What breaks without it.

WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE (2-3 sentences):
  Concrete description — specific enough to replicate.
  Include failure mode: "It breaks when [X]."
```

## Template 4: Short Take

```
Single strong opinion on a real, current event, decision, or product launch.
50-100 words. No padding.

FORMAT: [observation]. [why it's interesting or what it signals]. [your take if you have one].

Example:
"Figma's new AI generation feature buries the controls three clicks in.
That's a deliberate choice — they don't want feature-discovery to create expectation
of AI-first design. Makes sense. The risk is power users stop exploring.
I'd surface controls at the second click, not the third."
```

## Template 5: Career Milestone

**Use sparingly — max 1 per quarter. Only for genuinely significant events.**

```
STATE THE FACT first (no "excited to announce"):
  "Starting [role] at [company] on [date]."

WHY IT MATTERS TO YOU (2-3 sentences):
  Specific — what drew you to this company or problem.
  NOT: "I'm passionate about [generic domain]"
  YES: "[Company] is tackling [specific problem] in a way that [specific angle]."

ONE FORWARD-LOOKING SENTENCE:
  What you're going to work on or learn.
```

**Hard no:** Humble-bragging. "After X rejections…" stories. Gratitude lists. "Couldn't have done it without my incredible team."

## 4-Week Calendar Template

```
GOAL: [e.g., signal AI PM archetype before Anthropic interview]
SIGNALS TO FEATURE: [from linkright-mem or user input]

WEEK 1:
  Post 1 (POT):    [Company A] — [specific feature to research and analyze]
  Post 2 (Insight): [signal] — [specific angle or lesson]

WEEK 2:
  Post 3 (Short Take):  [recent industry event or product decision — research this]
  Post 4 (Process):     [how you approach one specific PM problem type]

WEEK 3:
  Post 5 (POT):    [Company B] — [different signal emphasis than Post 1]
  Post 6 (Insight): [framework or mental model you actually use]

WEEK 4:
  Post 7 (Short Take or Milestone): [if milestone exists; else short take]
  Post 8 (Insight): [pattern recognition — something you keep seeing across your work]

Frequency: 2 posts/week. 3/week max during peak application push.
Quality > quantity. One POT with real research > five generic insights.
```
