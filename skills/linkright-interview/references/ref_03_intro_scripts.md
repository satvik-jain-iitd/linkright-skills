# ref_03 — Intro Scripts (Tell Me About Yourself)

## The TMAY Framework

**T**ransition → **M**oment → **A**rchetype → **Y**our ask

| Part | What to cover | Length |
|---|---|---|
| Transition | Where you're coming from + why you're moving | 1-2 sentences |
| Moment | 1 concrete example of your most relevant signal | 2-3 sentences |
| Archetype | What kind of PM you are + what you bring | 1-2 sentences |
| Your ask | Why this specific company/role right now | 1 sentence |

Total target: 60-90 seconds (never >2 minutes). Conversational, not recited.

---

## Template by Archetype

### Growth PM

```
I've spent the last [N] years building and optimizing growth systems at [company type].
Most recently I [specific thing: "redesigned our onboarding flow and grew 30-day retention from X to Y"
or "built our referral program from scratch, which became our highest CAC-efficiency channel"].

I tend to operate at the intersection of data and user behavior — I care deeply about
the mechanism behind why users come back, not just whether they do.

I'm drawn to [this company / this role] because [specific reason tied to their growth stage or problem].
```

### 0→1 / Founding PM

```
I've spent most of my career figuring out what to build before figuring out how to build it.
At [company] I [founded / co-led / owned] a product from zero — [brief: what it was, what happened].

I work best in ambiguity: I'm the PM who writes the first doc, runs the first user interviews,
and pushes to ship before the product feels ready.

I'm here because [company] is at a stage where that instinct is actually useful rather than just noisy.
```

### Enterprise B2B PM

```
I've spent [N] years building products for [enterprise / B2B / regulated] customers —
the kind where the buyer, the user, and the economic decision-maker are three different people.

At [company] I [specific: "owned our top-10-account segment's product roadmap"
or "built the compliance features that unblocked 6 enterprise deals"].

I'm drawn to complex buying environments — understanding procurement, champions, and
the gap between what a customer asks for and what they actually need.

[Company]'s challenge around [specific thing] is exactly the kind of problem I want to work on next.
```

### Platform / Infrastructure PM

```
I've spent [N] years building products that other teams build on top of.
At [company] I [specific: "owned the API platform" or "rebuilt our data pipeline infrastructure"].

I think about platform PM work as solving two problems simultaneously:
the immediate developer experience and the long-term capability unlock.

I'm interested in [company] because [specific platform challenge they have that you can address].
```

### Consumer PM

```
I care about the moment a user actually enjoys using a product — not just the retention metric,
but the thing that caused it.

At [company] I [specific: "redesigned the core notification system, reducing opt-outs 40%"
or "built the sharing mechanics that became our main viral loop"].

I operate at the edge of product, psychology, and data — I want to understand
why people behave the way they do, then design for it.

[Company] is building [thing] and I think the hardest unsolved problem there is [your take].
That's why I'm here.
```

---

## Customization Rules

1. **Always name a metric or concrete outcome** in the Moment section — even a rough one
2. **Never list 3 things** — one strong example beats a tour of your resume
3. **Name the specific company** in the Your Ask section — "I'd love to join a growth-stage company" = fail
4. **First sentence is not your job title** — don't start with "I'm a PM at X"
5. **End with your take on their problem** if possible — shows research, not just interest

---

## Anti-Patterns

| What to avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "I'm a passionate, results-driven PM..." | Cliché opener, no information |
| Reciting resume in order | Not an intro, it's a reading |
| 3+ examples in 90 seconds | Too much, none land |
| "I've always loved products..." | Vague, every PM says this |
| No concrete outcome | All claim, no evidence |
| "I'm looking for my next challenge" | Explains nothing about you |

---

## Delivery Notes

- **Pause** after the Moment — let the interviewer react
- **Eye contact** at the start of each section
- **Slow down** on the metric — say the number clearly
- If the intro prompts a follow-up question, **go there first** rather than finishing the script
