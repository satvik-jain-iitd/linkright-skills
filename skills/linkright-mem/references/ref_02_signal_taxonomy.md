# ref_02 — Signal Taxonomy

## 4-Tier Structure

**Tier 1** — Core identity signals (who you are as a PM)
**Tier 2** — Functional depth signals (what you can actually do)
**Tier 3** — Context signals (environments you've operated in)
**Tier 4** — Evidence signals (proof backing Tier 1-3 claims)

---

## Tier 1 — Core Identity Signals

| Signal | Definition | Evidence threshold |
|---|---|---|
| `product_vision` | Ability to define where a product should go and why | Led strategy doc or roadmap that was adopted |
| `systems_thinking` | See interdependencies; reason about second-order effects | Designed multi-component system or flow |
| `user_empathy` | Deep understanding of user needs, behaviors, mental models | User research led to measurable design change |
| `outcome_ownership` | Takes accountability for results, not just delivery | Owns a metric that moved — good or bad |
| `ambiguity_tolerance` | Makes decisions and moves forward with incomplete info | Led decision under uncertainty, documented tradeoff |
| `influence_without_authority` | Moves people who don't report to you | Changed behavior of cross-functional partner |

---

## Tier 2 — Functional Depth Signals

| Signal | Definition | Evidence threshold |
|---|---|---|
| `discovery_rigor` | Structured process for problem definition before solutioning | Ran discovery that changed product direction |
| `prioritization_framework` | Repeatable method for deciding what to build next | Used framework (RICE, ICE, etc.) on real decision |
| `growth_experimentation` | Runs hypothesis-driven A/B or multivariate experiments | Designed experiment, read results, shipped or killed |
| `data_fluency` | Reads and acts on quantitative data without analyst dependency | Wrote SQL or analyzed data directly to make decision |
| `roadmap_ownership` | Sets and maintains a multi-quarter roadmap | Authored roadmap reviewed by leadership |
| `stakeholder_management` | Manages up, across, and down; navigates politics | Navigated multi-stakeholder conflict to ship |
| `technical_depth` | Understands system design, APIs, infra sufficiently to partner on architecture | Co-designed technical approach with engineering |
| `design_collaboration` | Works with design at wireframe/prototype stage, not just spec review | Partnered in design reviews that changed outcome |
| `go_to_market` | Understands launch strategy, positioning, pricing, channel | Contributed to launch plan that drove adoption |
| `metric_definition` | Defines north star, guardrail, and leading metrics for a product | Defined metrics framework used by team |

---

## Tier 3 — Context Signals

| Signal | Definition |
|---|---|
| `early_stage_experience` | Worked at company with <50 employees or in 0-to-1 stage |
| `enterprise_experience` | Shipped product for enterprise customers (procurement, compliance, champions) |
| `b2c_experience` | Built consumer-facing products with large user bases |
| `platform_experience` | Built internal platforms, APIs, or developer-facing products |
| `marketplace_experience` | Managed two-sided marketplace dynamics (supply/demand balance) |
| `regulated_industry` | Worked in fintech, health, legal, or other regulated domain |
| `remote_distributed_team` | Led cross-timezone product team |
| `high_growth_stage` | Operated during rapid scaling (10x users, team, or revenue) |
| `turnaround_context` | Joined or led product in a struggling or pivoting organization |
| `international_products` | Shipped product for non-home-country markets, handled localization |

---

## Tier 4 — Evidence Signals

Tier 4 signals are not standalone — they back Tier 1-3 claims.

| Signal | What it evidences |
|---|---|
| `metric_moved` | Outcome ownership, growth experimentation, data fluency |
| `shipped_0_to_1` | Early stage experience, ambiguity tolerance, product vision |
| `led_discovery_that_changed_direction` | Discovery rigor, user empathy |
| `managed_exec_stakeholder` | Stakeholder management, influence without authority |
| `promoted_at_company` | Outcome ownership, sustained performance |
| `built_with_no_team` | 0→1 experience, technical depth |
| `story_about_failure_owned` | Outcome ownership, ambiguity tolerance |
| `cross_functional_conflict_resolved` | Influence without authority, stakeholder management |

---

## Signal Strength Levels

| Level | Definition |
|---|---|
| `STRONG` | Multiple concrete examples, metric or outcome attached, mentioned in multiple job contexts |
| `MEDIUM` | One clear example, outcome not fully quantified, or limited to one role |
| `LOW` | Experience exists but no concrete story ready, or only supporting role |
| `UNKNOWN` | Not yet assessed — needs facts extraction |

---

## Signal Derivation Rules

When extracting signals from a fact:

1. One fact can derive multiple signals — list all that apply
2. Only assign `STRONG` if fact has: specific action + quantified result + role-appropriate context
3. Assign `MEDIUM` if fact has 2 of 3 above criteria
4. Assign `LOW` if fact has 1 or fewer — fact is still valid, signal is just weaker evidence
5. `UNKNOWN` is set during gap analysis — not derived from a fact
