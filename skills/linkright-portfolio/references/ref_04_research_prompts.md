# ref_04 — Company Research Prompts

## When to Use This File

Run company research before:
- Writing a POT (Proof-of-Thinking) for a target company
- Preparing for a first-round interview
- Deciding whether to apply to a role

Time investment: 20-30 minutes per company. Don't skip it.

---

## Research Checklist

### Product Research (10 min — use the actual product)

- [ ] Sign up for the product (or log in if existing user)
- [ ] Complete the core user flow — what does it actually do?
- [ ] Notice what's missing — what would you expect to be there but isn't?
- [ ] Find the most recently shipped feature (usually in changelog or blog)
- [ ] Identify the one flow that feels most polished vs least polished

### Company Research (10 min — public sources)

- [ ] Funding stage and amount (Crunchbase, company site)
- [ ] Approximate team size (LinkedIn company page)
- [ ] Core customers: who buys this? Who uses it?
- [ ] Recent news: last 90 days (company blog, TechCrunch, LinkedIn posts from their team)
- [ ] Job description for the role you're targeting — read every line

### PM Culture Research (5 min — optional but valuable)

- [ ] Read any published PM blog posts from their team (Medium, Substack, personal sites)
- [ ] Find interview reviews on Glassdoor or Blind — what do PMs say about the process?
- [ ] Check their public roadmap or changelog if available (e.g., Linear changelog, Notion updates page)
- [ ] Look at job description language — what signals does it emphasize?

## Research Brief Template

Fill this out before writing a POT or heading into a first interview.

```
COMPANY RESEARCH BRIEF — [Company Name]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]

PRODUCT OVERVIEW
  What: [1-2 sentences: what they sell and to whom]
  Stage: [seed / Series A-B / growth / public / enterprise]
  Revenue model: [subscription / usage / transaction fee / freemium / ads]

CORE PRODUCT DECISIONS (2-3 specific things you noticed in the product)
  1. [Specific observation — e.g., "No emoji reactions on issues"]
  2. [Specific observation — e.g., "Onboarding skips role selection entirely"]
  3. [Optional third]

KNOWN PM CHALLENGES (from JD + research)
  [What problems does the PM team appear to be working on?
   Pull from JD language, changelog, recent blog posts]

RECENT NEWS (last 90 days)
  [Funding / launch / pivot / team change / press]

WHAT KIND OF PM THEY SEEM TO VALUE
  [Based on JD language — what signals do they emphasize?
   e.g., "data fluency and experiment velocity", "design sensibility", "enterprise GTM"]

POT OPPORTUNITY
  Topic: [The ONE specific thing you noticed that would make a strong POT]
  Why this topic: [Why it reveals a real trade-off or decision point]
  Confidence: [HIGH / MEDIUM — do you have enough specifics to write well?]
```

---

## JD Analysis — What to Extract

When reading a job description, extract:

1. **Primary signals** — what does the first paragraph emphasize?
   - "Growth-focused" → `growth_experimentation` is required
   - "0 to 1" → `ambiguity_tolerance` + `early_stage_experience`
   - "Enterprise customers" → `enterprise_experience` + `stakeholder_management`

2. **Required tools/methods** — SQL, A/B testing, roadmap, Figma, API
   - Maps directly to `data_fluency`, `technical_depth`, `design_collaboration`

3. **Adjectives they use for candidates** — "scrappy", "data-driven", "customer-obsessed", "technical"
   - These reveal culture and screening criteria

4. **What's buried in section 3** (usually the actual pain point) — "you'll help us scale our..."

## POT Research Prompt (use with any research tool)

When searching for POT material on a specific company:

```
I'm preparing a Proof-of-Thinking analysis of [Company]'s product for a PM interview.
I've used the product recently.

Help me find:
1. Any specific product decisions they've made that have been publicly discussed (changelog, blog, PM posts)
2. Known UX or product trade-offs that their team has acknowledged
3. Recent product changes or feature launches
4. Any criticism or feature requests from users that they've addressed or declined

I want to write about ONE specific decision — not a general overview.
```

---

## Research Quality Check

Before starting to write a POT or entering an interview, answer these:

- [ ] Can I describe their core product in 2 sentences to someone who's never used it?
- [ ] Can I name one specific product decision they made and why?
- [ ] Do I know their current stage and business model?
- [ ] Have I actually used the product in the last 2 weeks?
- [ ] Do I know what kind of PM they're hiring for?

If any answer is NO: do more research before proceeding.
