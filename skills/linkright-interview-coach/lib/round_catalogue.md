# Round + Problem-Type Catalogue

User picks ROUND. Skill rolls PROBLEM TYPE within (via `shuf`). Problem type determines:
- Total duration (budget)
- Phase structure (names + sub-budgets)
- Opening question (random from PT's bank)

---

## Round 1 — HR Screening (~15min)

**Format**: 4 short phases. Recruiter-level intensity.
**Difficulty bar**: Recruiter screen
**Best for**: All career levels; warm-up

### PT 1.1 — Standard intro screen (15min)
- Phase 1: Background (3m)
- Phase 2: Why this company (4m)
- Phase 3: Compensation expectations (4m)
- Phase 4: Logistics (timeline, location, visa) (4m)

**Opening Qs (random pick)**:
- "Walk me through your background in 90 seconds — what brings you here?"
- "Tell me about yourself in 2 minutes."
- "Help me understand your career so far."

### PT 1.2 — Recruiter referral screen (15min)
- Phase 1: Referral context (3m)
- Phase 2: Background highlights (4m)
- Phase 3: Compensation (4m)
- Phase 4: Timeline + logistics (4m)

**Opening Qs**:
- "X referred you — what made you say yes when they reached out?"
- "Tell me what excites you about this team specifically."

### PT 1.3 — Cold-applied screen (15min)
- Phase 1: Background (4m)
- Phase 2: Why this role (4m)
- Phase 3: Compensation (4m)
- Phase 4: Visa / logistics (3m)

**Opening Qs**:
- "You applied directly — what drew you to this role?"
- "What's your current situation, and what are you optimizing for?"

## Round 2 — Behavioral / STAR (~25min)

**Format**: Story-driven; 1-2 prompts per session
**Difficulty bar**: Senior-level
**Best for**: Mid+ PMs

### PT 2.1 — Leadership without authority (25min)
- Phase 1: Story setup + situation (5m)
- Phase 2: Action details (8m)
- Phase 3: Outcome + metrics (5m)
- Phase 4: Learning + follow-up (7m)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Tell me about a time you led a project without formal authority over the team."
- "Walk me through a moment when you had to influence engineers to change direction without being their manager."

### PT 2.2 — Conflict with engineering / cross-functional (25min)
Same phase structure.

**Opening Qs**:
- "Tell me about a serious conflict with engineering — what was at stake and how did you resolve it?"
- "Describe a time when design and product disagreed sharply. Your role?"

### PT 2.3 — Biggest product failure + learning (25min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "What's the biggest product failure you've owned?"
- "Tell me about a launch that didn't go well — what would you do differently?"

### PT 2.4 — Influencing a senior stakeholder (25min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Tell me about a time you changed a senior leader's mind on a strategic call."
- "Walk me through a moment when you pushed back on your VP and how it played out."

### PT 2.5 — Handling ambiguity (25min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Tell me about a project where the scope was totally unclear when you started."
- "Describe a time you had to make a major decision with minimal data."

### PT 2.6 — Disagree-and-commit (25min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager but committed to their direction anyway. What happened?"
- "Walk me through a moment when you felt strongly against a leadership call but had to execute. How did you handle it?"

## Round 3 — Culture Fit / Values (~20min)

**Format**: 4 phases, conversational
**Difficulty bar**: Hiring manager
**Best for**: All career levels

### PT 3.1 — Resume deep-dive narrative (20min)
- Phase 1: Pick most-impactful project on resume (5m)
- Phase 2: Walk through situation + context (5m)
- Phase 3: Your specific contribution + decisions (5m)
- Phase 4: Outcome + reflection (5m)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Pick one project on your resume and walk me through it — like you're telling a teammate over coffee."
- "Looking at your last role, what's the project you'd want me to know about?"

### PT 3.2 — Values challenge (20min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "What's a strongly-held opinion you've changed in the last 12 months? What changed your mind?"
- "Tell me about a value you hold that has cost you something at work."

### PT 3.3 — Why this company specifically (20min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Why us, specifically — not the category, the company?"
- "What about our most recent quarter / product launch / strategy excites you, and why?"

### PT 3.4 — Reverse interview (20min)
- Phase 1: User asks Sage their first Q (5m)
- Phase 2-4: Quality of user's Qs evaluated (5m each)

**Opening Qs (Sage prompts)**:
- "Now your turn — what 3-5 Qs do you want to ask me?"
- "What do you want to know about this team / company / role?"

## Round 4 — Product Sense (variable 25-45min)

**Format**: Variable phase count per PT (5-7 phases)
**Difficulty bar**: Senior-level
**Best for**: All levels (PT-dependent)

### PT 4.1 — Favourite product (30min)
- Phase 1: Pitch (60-90s) (5m)
- Phase 2: Users + segmentation (5m)
- Phase 3: Moat / why this wins (5m)
- Phase 4: 3 problems + improve one (8m)
- Phase 5: Metrics + risks (7m)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Tell me a product you love. Pitch it in 60 seconds — what, who, why."
- "What's a product you use daily that you think is genuinely well-designed?"
- "Walk me through a product where you feel the team nailed something hard."

### PT 4.2 — Improve X (35min)
- Phase 1: Clarify scope (5m)
- Phase 2: Users + primary segment (5m)
- Phase 3: Top 3 pain points (5m)
- Phase 4: 2-3 solutions (8m)
- Phase 5: Tradeoffs + prioritize one (5m)
- Phase 6: Metrics (7m)

**Opening Qs**:
- "How would you improve LinkedIn Premium?"
- "How would you improve Zomato Pro?"
- "How would you improve Google Maps for Indian roads?"
- "How would you improve YouTube Shorts for creators?"

### PT 4.3 — Design X for Y (45min)
- Phase 1: Clarify objective + scope (5m)
- Phase 2: Users + segments (5m)
- Phase 3: JTBD for primary segment (7m)
- Phase 4: 3-5 solution directions (10m)
- Phase 5: Prioritize 1-2 (5m)
- Phase 6: Design details for chosen (6m)
- Phase 7: Metrics + risks (7m)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Design a product for college students preparing for placements in India."
- "Design WhatsApp for senior citizens (60+) in tier-2 cities."
- "Design a learning product for kids aged 6-10."
- "Design a financial-planning product for gig workers in India."

### PT 4.4 — Diagnose drop (30min)
- Phase 1: Clarify metric + scope (3m)
- Phase 2: Hypothesis tree (8m)
- Phase 3: Prioritize hypotheses (5m)
- Phase 4: Investigation plan (7m)
- Phase 5: Likely action (7m)

**Opening Qs**:
- "YouTube watch time dropped 8% week-over-week. What would you do?"
- "Uber driver supply dropped 15% in Bangalore over 30 days. Walk me through your investigation."
- "Instagram Stories views are down 12% for users 18-24. Where do you start?"

### PT 4.5 — Market sizing (25min)
- Phase 1: Clarify scope (3m)
- Phase 2: Structure the estimate (7m)
- Phase 3: Numbers + math (8m)
- Phase 4: Sanity check + sensitivity (7m)

**Opening Qs**:
- "How many cricket balls are sold in India every year?"
- "Estimate annual revenue of all cafes in Mumbai."
- "How many hours of YouTube are watched in India daily?"

## Round 5 — Analytical / Diagnostic (~30min)

**Format**: 4-5 phases
**Difficulty bar**: Senior-level
**Best for**: Mid+ PMs

### PT 5.1 — Metric design (30min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Define success metrics for a new dating app."
- "How would you measure whether Google Maps is 'working' for a user?"

### PT 5.2 — A/B test critique (30min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Here's an A/B test result: variant B shows +5% click-through, p=0.03, n=10K per arm, 7-day duration. Would you ship it? Walk me through your thinking."
- "We A/B tested a new onboarding flow: conversion up 15% but D7 retention down 8%. What would you do?"

### PT 5.3 — Diagnose unexpected drop (30min)
(See PT 4.4 — overlap allowed)

### PT 5.4 — Prioritize 5 features with limited engineering (30min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Here are 5 features, here's our 6-engineer team and a Q4 deadline. Prioritize."
- "Your CEO wants A, B, C all shipped in 8 weeks but you can ship 1.5 of them. How do you decide and what do you tell the CEO?"

## Round 7 — Bar Raiser (~35min)

**Format**: Curveball + LP drill structure
**Difficulty bar**: Principal / E7
**Best for**: Senior+ PMs

### PT 7.1 — Hard product Q + Ownership LP (35min)
- Phase 1: Hard product Q (10m)
- Phase 2: LP drill — Ownership (10m)
- Phase 3: Curveball (10m)
- Phase 4: Reverse Qs (5m)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Design a product for X. After Phase 1, I'll drill into Ownership stories."

### PT 7.2 — Strategy under uncertainty (35min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Your company's biggest competitor just raised $200M and announced direct entry into your category. You're the product lead. What's your 30-60-90?"

### PT 7.3 — Curveball (35min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Your EM just quit a week before your biggest launch. Engineering is panicking. What do you do?"
- "QA found a P0 bug 12 hours before launch. CEO wants to ship anyway. Walk me through it."

### PT 7.4 — Customer obsession deep-dive (35min)

**Opening Qs**:
- "Tell me about the lowest-NPS user segment you've served — what did you do for them?"
- "Walk me through a moment when you advocated for users against your own business case."
