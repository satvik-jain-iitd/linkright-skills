# Cold Email Templates — LinkRight Network

## Core Principles

Cold emails fail for one reason: they ask the recipient to care before giving them a reason to.
Every email needs one thing that proves you did homework — a specific observation about their work,
their company, or their published thinking. Generic openers ("I'm a big fan") signal exactly the
opposite: you didn't do homework.

---

## Email 1: Hiring Manager Cold Outreach

**Goal:** Get a 20-minute call or an invitation to apply directly.

```
SUBJECT: [specific topic] — quick question from a [brief signal]

Examples:
  "AI onboarding flows — question from a PM building in this space"
  "Enterprise workflow design — curiosity from an AmEx PM"
  NOT: "Would love to connect" / "Quick intro" / "Job inquiry"

BODY:

Para 1 (observation — 2-3 sentences):
  Specific observation about their product, team, or published work.
  Shows you did actual research, not flattery.

  Template:
  "I noticed [company]'s [feature/decision] takes a different approach than [comparison point] —
  [what you observed]. [What the tradeoff seems to be, in one sentence]."

  OR for someone who's written/spoken publicly:
  "Your [post/talk/interview] on [specific topic] surfaced something I've been thinking about —
  [specific point they made] and [why it connected to your own work/thinking]."

Para 2 (who you are — 1-2 sentences):
  Role + domain + why their specific work is relevant.
  "[X] years in [domain], currently [what you're building/doing].
  Your [team/work/problem] sits squarely in what I'm focused on."

Para 3 (ask — 1-2 sentences):
  Specific, easy to say yes to.
  "Would you have 20 minutes for a call sometime in the next 2 weeks?
  Happy to send one question by email if that's easier."

NO CLOSING FILLER. Sign with name + LinkedIn URL only.
```

**Forbidden:**
- "I'd love to pick your brain"
- "I'm a big fan of your company / mission"
- "I know you're busy, but..."
- "I'm reaching out because..." (wastes the first sentence)
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "I'm sure you get a lot of emails like this"

## Email 2: Referral Request (warm connection at target company)

**Goal:** Ask for a referral or intro to the hiring team.

```
SUBJECT: [company] — would you be comfortable referring me?

BODY:

Para 1 (personal connection acknowledgement — 1 sentence):
  Reference how you know each other. Specific, not performative.
  "We met at [event] / worked together at [company] / you helped me with [X]."

Para 2 (what you're going for — 2-3 sentences):
  Specific role + why this company specifically.
  "I'm interested in the [role] at [company]. [One specific thing about the company/team
  that drew you — not generic mission language]."

Para 3 (the ask + what you're sending them — 2-3 sentences):
  Make it zero effort for them.
  "Would you be comfortable referring me if you think I'm a fit?
  I'm attaching a one-page overview of my experience and the role I'm targeting.
  [If they say yes, I'll handle the full application — you'd just submit the referral link]."

NO ask for "a call to catch up" as a pretext for the referral ask.
If you want the referral, ask for the referral.
```

## Email 3: Informational Interview Ask

**Goal:** Learn about a company/team; no hidden job ask (be genuine about this).

```
SUBJECT: Question about [specific topic] at [company] — 20 min?

BODY:

Para 1 (specific curiosity — 2-3 sentences):
  One specific thing you genuinely want to understand — not "how to break in."
  "I'm trying to understand how [company] structures [specific thing — e.g., PM-engineering
  collaboration on platform teams]. Your background on [team/product] makes you someone
  whose perspective would be valuable."

Para 2 (brief who you are — 1-2 sentences):
  One sentence. Not a full bio.
  "I'm a PM with [X years] in [domain], currently [transition you're in or project you're working on]."

Para 3 (ask):
  "Would you be open to a 20-minute call? I'll prepare specific questions in advance."

IMPORTANT: If you're job hunting, be honest if asked. Don't pretend you're "just curious"
if you're hoping they forward your resume. Most people can tell, and it damages trust.
It's fine to say: "I'm also exploring opportunities at [company] — but genuinely want to
learn about [specific thing] regardless of where that goes."
```

---

## Email Format Rules

**Subject line:** Specific topic first, then brief identity signal. Never a bare question.

**Length:** 120-180 words total. Three paragraphs, never four.

**Tone:** Peer, not supplicant. You are approaching them as someone who has done research,
has specific questions, and respects their time. Not as someone asking for a favor.

**Follow-up (if no reply after 7 days):**
One-sentence bump. Reply to original email thread.
"Bumping this in case it got buried — happy to connect whenever works for you."
Send once only. No second bump.

## Email to Avoid

```
Hi [Name],

I hope this email finds you well! I'm reaching out because I'm a huge fan of [Company]
and have been following your incredible work for a while. I believe I would be a
great fit for your team and would love to pick your brain about opportunities.

I have X years of experience in [domain] and am passionate about [generic thing].
I've attached my resume for your reference.

Looking forward to connecting!

Best,
[Name]
```

Why this fails:
- Opens with filler ("I hope this email finds you well")
- Generic flattery ("huge fan", "incredible work")
- No specific observation about their actual work
- "Pick your brain" — vague ask
- "I believe I would be a great fit" — for the recipient to evaluate, not you to assert
- Closes with nothing — no specific ask, no easy way to respond
